About Chiropractic Science
The Chiropractic profession is the world's largest complementary healthcare solution that is proven effective in treating musculoskeletal conditions without the use of drugs or surgeries. It focuses on treating the root causes of potential issues rather than just the symptoms.
The study of Chiropractic Science specialises in the important structures of the body, with a strong focus on the spine. The spinal cord - the main pathway of the nervous system - is located within the canal space of the spine and functions as the nerve superhighway, transmitting signals between the brain and the rest of the body. All bodily functions and movements are essentially linked by this system.
As such, a subluxation or misalignment of the spine can affect the spinal nerve as it exits the spinal cord, leading to many common physical wellbeing issues - conditions like headache, pain the neck or the lower back, numbness or tingling in extremities, slipped disc, and many others. A chiropractic adjustment helps to realign the spine which releases pressure on pinched nerves and other irritations, thereby providing relief.

Dr Neo Zhewei
Chiropractor | Wellness Champion | Sports Enthusiast | Avid Cyclist
Dr Neo Zhewei graduated from Murdoch University Chiropractic program in Western Australia, Perth. He has practiced in Perth and Singapore for six years before setting up his own clinic. Dr Neo is skilled in a variety of corrective Chiropractic techniques including the Diversified Technique and the Sacro-Occipital Technique (SOT). Dr Neo is an avid cyclist and goes on regular road biking trips with his cycling group. He has completed many century rides (over 160km) in Kuantan, Malacca and Rembau. Some Words From Dr Neo
"My chiropractic journey began at the age of nineteen when I was conscripted into the Singapore army. It was in Officer Cadet School where I experienced severe lower back pain during outfield training. I didn’t think much of it as I thought the pain would go away after some rest, but it didn’t. The next few days, I was having difficulty getting out of bed and was unable to continue with the training.
The Medical Officer gave me painkillers and muscle relaxants; while it did help reduce the pain, the injury relapsed once I was off medication. I was getting worried because if too many training sessions were missed, I would not be able to commission as an Officer of the Singapore Armed Forces.
I was referred to an Orthopaedic specialist, where I was diagnosed with disc protrusion (commonly known as “slipped disc”) and was recommended surgery - an option that I thought should be a last resort. After the visit, I decided to try alternatives like TCM and Acupuncture. Unfortunately, those treatments only gave me temporary relief.
This was when I visited a Chiropractor. After a few chiropractic adjustments, my lower back felt much better, and the pain did not relapse. I realized the dramatic improvements that good Chiropractic treatments provide - how it allows the body to utilise its innate self-healing ability - and have been seeing my Chiropractor ever since.
It was this life-changing experience that prompted me to study the science of Chiropractic methodologies, become an accredited Chiropractor myself, and helping the community achieve their wellness goals."
